The Core Principle
Email enrichment costs money — per contact, per API call. If you enrich 97 raw LinkedIn engagers, you'll pay for 60+ people who are agency owners, ghostwriters, and service providers who will never buy. Session 2 is the gate that runs before any paid enrichment: it qualifies, scores, and deduplicates for free — so Session 3 only spends on leads worth it.

Step 01
Headline Filter
run_javascript Free · No API

Regex-based pass/fail against the LinkedIn headline. Requires at least one founder signal (CEO, Co-founder, Founder). Excludes non-ICP roles regardless of founder title: Founder's Office staff, ghostwriters, SDRs, account managers, LinkedIn ad specialists.

icp_headline_pass headline_filter_reason has_saas_signal
Step 02
AI ICP Scoring
Claude Haiku Free · No Credits

Claude reads each headline and scores ICP fit 0–10 against the Northbound ICP: B2B SaaS founders at $0–10M ARR, pre-seed to Series A, where the founder IS the GTM team. Catches nuance keywords can't — an "AI-Native GTM Agency" founder scores 2/10 even with strong SaaS signals, because they're a service provider.

score score_label fit_band rationale is_saas_founder
Step 03
RevyOps Dedup Check
run_javascript Free · BYO API Key

Checks every LinkedIn URL against the RevyOps master contacts list before queueing for enrichment. Prevents re-enriching leads already in the CRM — or worse, re-sending cold outreach to someone already mid-sequence. Zero Deepline credits; calls RevyOps REST API directly.

Why filter first? The cost math.
Without Session 2

Enrich all 97 leads at ~$0.05–0.15 per contact across LeadMagic + Prospeo + Exa. You spend $15–45 enriching agency founders, ghostwriters, and SDRs who will never convert. Those credits are gone — and your campaign is polluted with low-quality contacts.

VS
With Session 2

Filter for free, then enrich only the ~30 leads with 6+ ICP scores. Enrichment spend drops by 60–70%. Every contact entering Session 3 has a clear ICP rationale attached, so copy personalisation in Session 4 has real signal to work from — not just a name and a job title.
